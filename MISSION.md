# Mission

## Knowledge Manufacturing Reference Model (KMRM)

The mission of KMRM is to define a stable, implementation-independent reference model for transforming raw knowledge into traceable, validated, and reusable knowledge products.

KMRM exists to help humans and AI systems handle knowledge with discipline: extract it faithfully, structure it clearly, preserve its provenance, validate its relationships, and manufacture reliable outputs without inventing or losing meaning.

## Core Purpose

KMRM provides the conceptual foundation for systems that need to turn dispersed knowledge sources into finished knowledge products such as manuals, curricula, support guides, policies, training materials, AI context packages, and other structured outputs.

It does not define one application, one repository, one organisation, or one AI agent system. It defines the underlying model that many implementations may follow.

## Mission Statement

KMRM shall establish a common language, structure, and lifecycle for knowledge manufacturing so that any conforming implementation can:

1. identify raw knowledge sources;
2. convert them into structured knowledge objects;
3. preserve provenance and traceability;
4. relate knowledge objects into a usable graph;
5. validate completeness, consistency, and source grounding;
6. assemble knowledge products through repeatable recipes;
7. package outputs for different formats and use cases; and
8. maintain outputs as source knowledge changes.

## Scope

KMRM covers:

- knowledge objects;
- knowledge sources;
- provenance;
- traceability;
- relationships;
- validation;
- manufacturing stages;
- production recipes;
- lifecycle states;
- quality gates; and
- knowledge products.

KMRM does not prescribe:

- a specific programming language;
- a specific database;
- a specific AI model;
- a specific user interface;
- a specific product domain; or
- a specific implementation architecture.

## Guiding Principle

KMRM treats knowledge as a manufacturing input, not as loose text. Every finished knowledge product should be traceable back to its source, reproducible through a defined process, and reviewable through explicit quality gates.

## Relationship to Document Factory

Document Factory is expected to be one possible implementation of KMRM. KMRM defines the reference model; Document Factory may implement that model to manufacture documentation, training, media, and support outputs.

## Relationship to Product Repositories

Product repositories such as GIA, church study materials, organisational workflows, or future applications are not the source of KMRM itself. They are validation grounds and source systems that may be connected to a KMRM-compliant implementation through adapters.
